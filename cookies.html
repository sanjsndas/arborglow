1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide useful information to website owners.

Session Cookies

Temporary cookies that remain in your browser's cookie file until you leave the site. These enable certain website functionality like maintaining your logged-in state as you navigate between pages. Session cookies don't collect information from your computer and are deleted when you close your browser.

Example: Keeping items in your shopping cart during one browsing session.

Persistent Cookies

These cookies remain on your device for a set period (usually between minutes to multiple years) unless manually deleted. They remember your preferences and actions across multiple visits, improving site functionality and user experience.

Example: Remembering your language preference when you return to our site.

First-Party Cookies

Set by the website you're visiting directly. These are generally considered safer as they can only be read by that specific site. We use first-party cookies for authentication, security, and remembering your selections.

Example: Remembering you've seen our cookie banner so it doesn't reappear.

5. Managing Cookies

You have several options to control or limit how cookies are used on your devices:

5.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to manage cookie preferences through their settings. Typically, you can find these controls under "Options" or "Preferences" menus. Common browser settings allow you to:

  • Delete all existing cookies
  • Block all cookies (though this may break website functionality)
  • Allow only first-party cookies
  • Clear cookies when you close your browser
  • View and selectively delete specific cookies

5.2 Industry Opt-Out Tools

For advertising cookies specifically, several industry organizations provide opt-out mechanisms:

  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA): https://optout.aboutads.info/
  • European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance (EDAA): https://www.youronlinechoices.com/
  • Network Advertising Initiative (NAI): https://optout.networkadvertising.org/

These tools use cookies themselves to remember your opt-out preferences, so if you clear your cookies, you'll need to opt-out again.

6. Specific Browser Instructions

Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

Google Chrome

  1. Click the three-dot menu > Settings
  2. Select "Privacy and security" > "Cookies and other site data"
  3. Choose your preferred cookie settings
  4. To delete existing cookies: "See all cookies and site data"

Mozilla Firefox

  1. Click the menu button > Options
  2. Select "Privacy & Security" panel
  3. Under "Cookies and Site Data," choose settings
  4. Click "Manage Data" to view/remove specific cookies
