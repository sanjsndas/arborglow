Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about contacting ArborGlow and scheduling our services.

1. How quickly can I get an appointment for a consultation?

Standard consultations are typically scheduled within 3-5 business days, depending on seasonality. During peak periods (spring and fall), wait times may extend to 7-10 business days. We maintain some same-day emergency consultation slots for urgent situations involving hazardous trees. Our customer service team can provide current availability when you call or submit a request.

2. What information should I have ready when I call?

To help us serve you efficiently, please have the following information available: your property address, the number and types of trees needing attention, any specific concerns (like visible decay or leaning), your availability for an assessment, and any relevant photos you can share electronically. For emergency calls, we'll need details about the immediate hazard.

3. Do you charge for consultations?

Basic consultations within our standard service area are free for residential properties. There is a $150 consultation fee for commercial properties or residential properties outside our standard service area, which is credited toward any services you book with us. Detailed diagnostic reports or assessments requiring laboratory analysis may incur additional fees, which we'll discuss upfront.

4. What are your office hours?

Our main office at 238 1st Ave is open Monday-Friday 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM and Saturdays 9:00 AM to 2:00 PM. Phone support is available Monday-Friday 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM and Saturdays 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Emergency calls are answered 24/7. Field crews typically work Monday-Friday 7:30 AM to 4:30 PM, with some weekend availability during peak seasons.

5. How do I know if I need emergency services?

Call our emergency line immediately if you observe: large broken or hanging branches, trees that have fallen or are leaning significantly, trees that have damaged structures or vehicles, exposed roots indicating possible instability, or any situation where people or property are at immediate risk. When in doubt, it's better to call - our team can assess whether the situation requires emergency response.

6. Can I get an estimate over the phone?

While we can provide general price ranges for common services, accurate estimates require an on-site assessment by one of our arborists. Tree work costs depend on many factors including tree size, condition, location, accessibility, and local regulations. We're happy to provide ballpark figures for budgeting purposes, but these are not binding quotes.

7. What payment methods do you accept?

We accept all major credit cards (Visa, MasterCard, American Express, Discover), checks, bank transfers, and cash (for amounts under $1,000). Payment is due upon completion of services unless prior arrangements have been made. Commercial accounts may qualify for net-30 terms with approved credit. We offer financing options through third-party providers for larger projects.

8. Do I need to be home for the consultation?

While it's ideal for you to be present during the consultation so our arborist can discuss their findings directly with you, it's not strictly necessary. If you can't be present, please provide any specific instructions or concerns in advance, and ensure our team has access to all relevant areas of your property. We'll provide a detailed written report after the assessment.
